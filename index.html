1. High-Protein Soya Masala Rice (Lunch Base)

Focus: $\mathbf{50\text{g}}$ Soya Chunks + Free Rice. High satiety for the afternoon walk.

Ingredients:

  • $\mathbf{50\text{g}}$ Soya Chunks (dry weight)
  • $1 \text{ cup}$ Pre-cooked Rice (Free)
  • $50\text{g}$ Onion/Tomato/Green Chili (Budget Veg)
  • Oil/Ghee, Ginger-Garlic Paste, Masalas (Free)

Method:

  1. Soak Soya Chunks in hot water for 15 min, squeeze out water thoroughly, and set aside.
  2. Heat free oil/ghee, saut√© chopped onion, then add spices and ginger-garlic paste.
  3. Add Soya Chunks and cook until lightly browned (this enhances texture).
  4. Mix in pre-cooked Free Rice, chopped tomato, and salt. Cook for $5\text{ minutes}$ on low heat.

2. Double-Protein Dal & Egg Curry (Dinner Base)

Focus: $\mathbf{2 \text{ Eggs}} + \mathbf{100\text{g}}$ Dal. High protein for muscle recovery overnight.

Ingredients:

  • $\mathbf{2 \text{ Eggs}}$ (Budget)
  • $\mathbf{100\text{g}}$ Dal (pre-cooked) (Budget)
  • $50\text{g}$ Onion/Tomato/Green Chili (Budget Veg)
  • Atta for $\mathbf{4 \text{ Rotis}}$ (Free)
  • Oil/Ghee, Spices (Free)

Method:

  1. Boil the eggs, peel them, and keep aside.
  2. Prepare a basic curry base: heat oil/ghee, saut√© chopped onion, tomato, and spices.
  3. Add the $\mathbf{100\text{g}}$ of pre-cooked Dal and a splash of water (if needed). Simmer for $5\text{ minutes}$.
  4. Gently place the boiled eggs into the curry. Serve hot with fresh **Free Rotis**.

3. High-Protein Moong Dal Cheela

Uses: Dal allocation. Excellent quick, high-protein breakfast or snack.

Method:

  1. Soak $50\text{g}$ **Moong Dal** (Budget) overnight.
  2. Blend the soaked dal with a small green chili, ginger, and water to a smooth, flowing batter.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan (with minimal free oil/ghee) and spread the batter thinly like a dosa.
  4. Cook on both sides until golden. Serve with free mint/coriander chutney (if available).

4. Raw Soya Sprout Salad (Budget Snack)

Uses: Dal allocation. Raw, zero-oil, maximizes nutrients and fiber.

Method:

  1. Soak $\mathbf{50\text{g}}$ **Chana Dal** (Budget) or whole Moong dal overnight until soft.
  2. Drain and mix with chopped onion, tomato, and cucumber (Budget Veg).
  3. Add salt, pepper, and a dash of lemon juice (if available).
  4. This makes a quick, fibre-packed snack that you can eat before or after your walk.

5. High-Protein Dal Tikki (Using Leftovers)

Uses: Leftover Dal and Potato (Budget Veg). Highly satisfying evening snack.

Method:

  1. Mash $1$ small boiled potato (Budget Veg) with $100\text{g}$ of leftover cooked **Dal**.
  2. Add chopped coriander/chili and free spices (salt, *amchur*).
  3. Form into small patties (tikkis).
  4. Shallow-fry on a non-stick pan with minimal free oil/ghee until crisp and golden brown.

Remember the Golden Rule:

The high protein from the Dal, Soya, and Eggs is vital. Do NOT reduce these amounts to meet your budget; reduce the *free* rice/roti if needed.
